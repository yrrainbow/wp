Background

What happens now?

In recent years, the problem of hair loss has become increasingly severe and has become a global health challenge. Globally it is reported that the population suffering from severe hair loss has reached 2 billion (1 in 35), while in China, the number has exceeded 250 million. On average, 1 in every 6 people is troubled by hair loss in their lifetime, and there is a trend of younger age. Among various types of hair loss, seborrheic alopecia is the most common type, accounting for approximately 90% of the total number of hair loss patients.

For patients with seborrheic alopecia, due to the conversion of the androgen testosterone into dihydrotestosterone (DHT) under the action of 5α - reductase, DHT can bind to the androgen receptor (AR) in dermal papilla cells (DPCs) of the hair follicle, inducing hair follicle miniaturization, shortening the hair growth cycle, causing the hair to gradually become thinner, softer, and fall out, which seriously affects the appearance and mental health of patients.

Figure 1: Dermal papilla cells (DPCs) in the hair follicle.

Patients with alopecia areata present with suddenly appearing round or oval hair loss patches with clear boundaries, which can expand rapidly in a short period of time, bringing great mental stress to patients. Telogen effluvium is usually related to factors such as endocrine disorders, malnutrition, and mental stress. Patients experience a phenomenon where a large amount of hair simultaneously enters the telogen phase and falls out.

Figure 2: Many people suffer from hair loss.

Conventional Hair Loss Treatment

At present, the hair loss treatment methods on the market are mainly divided into drug therapy, surgical treatment, and physical therapy, etc.

Drug Therapy

In terms of drug therapy, topical medications such as minoxidil and oral medications such as finasteride are commonly used treatment means. However, these drugs have certain limitations. Minoxidil needs to be used for a long time to maintain the effect, and some patients may experience adverse reactions such as scalp itching and redness. Although finasteride has a certain effect on seborrheic alopecia, it may cause side effects such as sexual dysfunction, which limits its widespread application.

Surgical treatment

Surgical treatment mainly refers to hair transplantation. By transplanting healthy hair follicles from the occipital area to the hair loss area, the appearance can be improved. However, hair transplantation surgery is costly and has certain risks, such as infection and low hair follicle survival rate.

Physical therapies

Physical therapies such as laser therapy and massage have relatively limited effects and can only play an auxiliary role in treatment.

What's left to be solved?

Develop a safe, effective treatment method with fewer side effects.
Enhance the pertinence of treatment and formulate effective treatment plans for certain type of hair loss.
Reduce the treatment cost so that more patients can afford it.
Improve the convenience of treatment and reduce the treatment pain and time cost for patients.

Key Goals

(1) Precision Therapy for seborrheic alopecia: to achieve a long - acting and targeted drug - targeting treatment for seborrheic alopecia.

(2) Cell - engineered mass production of drugs: Achieve the design of gene pathways and the design of industrial mass - production models based on cell - free systems and Escherichia coli.

(3) Dissolvable microneedles: Achieve specific treatment for alopecia diseases at fixed points and in fixed areas of the scalp, relieve patients' pain and economic burden, and enhance the therapeutic effect of drug delivery.

Inspirations

(1) iGEM Projects

In 2013, Taxi.coli: Smart Drug Delivery by EPF Lausanne was launched. The team developed a concept of biosynthetic drug delivery which engineered Escherichia coli capable of secreting gelatinase. This enabled the E. coli to bind to gelatin nanoparticles through biotin - streptavidin interactions and release them at the corresponding sites, thus achieving drug delivery.

Figure 3: Many people suffer from hair loss.

In 2024, the HainanU - China team from Hainan University carried out the project GHK - Cu: Hair Expecto Patronum The team efficiently expressed copper peptides, which have the effect of repairing hair follicles and treating hair loss, in engineered bacteria through tandem repeat expression. They also constructed a bacterial community ecosystem with controllable population size using quorum sensing to improve production efficiency. Moreover, they utilized microfluidic technology to form microemulsions encapsulating copper peptides for product presentation.

Figure 4: "The woonderful use of copper peptides"

In 2024, the BioTarget project from the JLU - NBBMS team from Jilin University combined RNAi technology, bacterial therapy, and nanomedicine. By using modified attenuated Salmonella, the team precisely knocked down drug - resistant genes through trans - kingdom RNAi technology. A large amount of RGD peptides were expressed on the surface of the bacteria to enhance its tumor - targeting ability. Meanwhile, MPEG - PLG - PLL nanoparticles were prepared to encapsulate chemotherapeutic drugs, which were then linked to the cell wall of the engineered bacteria to assist in drug delivery.

Figure 5: BioTarget poster.

(2) Work Outside iGEM

Microneedles are tiny, painless needles that can penetrate the skin's outer layer to deliver drugs directly into the underlying tissues. This technology has revolutionized the way drugs are administered, especially for topical and transdermal applications. The ability of microneedles to bypass the skin's natural barriers and deliver drugs in a controlled manner has been a major inspiration for our project. We saw the potential of using soluble microneedles to deliver the peptides and RNA produced by our engineered bacteria directly to the hair follicles, increasing their bioavailability and effectiveness.

Figure 6: What a microneedle looks like and how work.

LNP delivery systems have also inspired us. Liposomes are spherical vesicles composed of lipid bilayers that can encapsulate drugs and deliver them to specific cells or tissues. Their ability to protect drugs from degradation and target specific sites in the body has been well - documented. We considered how we could adapt the concept of liposome - mediated delivery to our project, ensuring that the peptides and RNA remain stable during delivery and reach the hair follicles intact.

Figure 7: LNP delivery systems.

Project Overview

(1) Our Solutions

In order to treat seborrheic alopecia, we have developed a drug delivery system based on microneedles. We load AbTAC and keratinocyte growth factor - 2 (KGF - 2) onto the tips of the microneedles, and then deliver the drugs near the hair follicle cells through the microneedles, enabling them to exert their effects. This achieves targeted treatment of hair follicle cells and serves the purpose of treating hair loss.

· Cure Module

AbTAC is a recombinant bispecific antibody that can recruit E3 ligases to promote the ubiquitination and degradation of target proteins. Its structure consists of an E3 ligase ligand, a target protein ligand, and a linker. In this project, AbTAC targets type Ⅱ 5α - reductase, which is only present in the prostate, skin, and hair follicles, offering high targeting specificity. We selected RNF43 as the E3 ligase (it has a transmembrane structure and the RNF family is commonly used). When AbTAC is released near hair follicle cells, the target protein ligand of AbTAC binds to type Ⅱ 5α - reductase on the membrane, and the E3 ligase ligand binds to RNF43, leading to the ubiquitination of type Ⅱ 5α - reductase. After endocytosis, it fuses with lysosomes and is finally degraded.

Figure 8: AbTAC's working mechanism.

· Growth Module

Keratinocyte growth factor (KGF, also known as FGF - 7) belongs to the fibroblast growth factor family. It is produced by stromal cells, and its receptor KGFR is mainly distributed in epithelial tissues. It can stimulate the growth of epidermal units in the skin, such as hair follicles, sebaceous glands, and sweat glands. KGF promotes the keratinization of squamous cells and hair growth, and stimulates the proliferation and differentiation of hair follicle stem cells and hair matrix cells, as well as hair follicle development. Studies have found that hair follicles contain KGF receptors. KGF can protect hair follicles in the anagen and telogen phases from X - ray damage, increasing the survival rate of hair follicles by 14%. It can also protect hair follicle keratinocytes from damage caused by some chemotherapeutic drugs.

Figure 9: KGF(FGF-7)'s working mechanism.

· Microneedle Module

Adopt the soluble microneedle technology as a drug delivery system. The soluble microneedles are made of biodegradable materials, and their surfaces are coated with drugs and nanovsicles produced by the engineered bacteria. The microneedles can painlessly penetrate the skin's stratum corneum and directly deliver the drugs to the tissues around the hair follicles, improving the bioavailability of the drugs.

Future Prospects

Figure 12: China's anti-hair loss market with great potential.

(1) Treatment Prospects

Our anti - hair - loss treatment project holds broad prospects. The drugs and growth factors produced by engineered bacteria, combined with the soluble microneedle delivery system, are expected to offer a safe, effective and convenient treatment approach for individuals suffering from hair loss. This method can not only address common hair - loss types like seborrheic alopecia, but also be customized according to the specific conditions of different patients. This customization enhances the treatment's pertinence and effectiveness. As the project progresses further and clinical research unfolds, we are confident that this treatment method will bring new hope to those with hair - loss problems.

(2) Generalizability

The engineered bacteria and soluble microneedle technology designed in this project possess excellent generalizability. Engineered bacteria, functioning as a bioreactor, can express diverse bioactive molecules via gene - editing technology. These molecules can be used for treating other diseases or developing other biological products. The soluble microneedle technology, as a novel drug - delivery system, is not limited to hair - loss treatment. It can also be applied in treating other skin diseases, vaccine administration and other fields. Through further optimization and improvement of these design elements, their application scope can be expanded, providing support for research and development in a wider range of fields.

Challenge

(1) Technical Defects

How to achieve large - scale and stable production is a challenge. The preparation process of soluble microneedles also needs to be further optimized to improve the quality of microneedles and their drug - loading capacity.

(2) Ethical and Safety Defects

It is necessary to ensure that engineered bacteria do not cause harm to humans and the environment, and the use of bioactive molecules complies with ethical norms.

(3) Defects in Commercial Promotion

In terms of commercial promotion, the project faces challenges of market competition and consumer awareness. There are already a variety of anti - hair - loss products on the market. How to highlight the advantages of our products and attract consumers' attention and trust is a key issue.
